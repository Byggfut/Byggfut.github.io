What Does the Word "semantic" actually mean?

According to dictionary.com it is "of, relating to, or arising from the different meanings of words or symbols"

What are the Semantic Structures of HTML5?

  • HTML semantic structures are fundamental to HTML code
  • The word "semantic elements" quite literally means "elements with a meaning"
  • "Non-semantic" elements such as the div and span don't tell anything about content

The Basic Structure of Semantic Design

Semantic Design is Organized like a Newspaper

Headers: The Headlines

  • Like the headline of a newspaper, header identifies the theme of the content
  • According to th ebook "HTML and CSS3 for the Real World" a header is "a group of introductory or navigational aids.
  • They are often used as ways of introducing the content
  • Headings are designated with the elements h1 to h6. The smaller the element, the larger the heading.

Nav: the Index

  • Like the index of a newspaper, the nav section of a website guides the user where to go
  • Often used to house various links to other portions of the website
  • Navigation portions are designated by the "nav" tag

Section: The Hard News

  • Represents a generic section of a document or application
  • Can be compared to the "hard news" or the most up to date information that requires background
  • Examples include terms of service, "about" or mission statement sections, or specific blog entries or news stories
  • Essentially, anywhere there are long blocks of text
  • Must be semantic of meaningful to the overall theme of the website
  • Identified by the tag "section"

Article

  • Very imilar to the section tag
  • The only difference is that section is thematically related to the rest of the page
  • Article can be thematically completely seperate from the rest of the page
  • Like the feature story in a newspaper, it goes into depth and incldues background regarding the news story
  • A good rule of thumb when deciding whether or not to use an article or section is to consider a question. Could it be published independently without modification?
  • An artcile is identified by the tag "article"

Aside: Editorial Columns

  • Represents a part that's "tangentially related to the conetnt aroyhnd the aise element, and which could be conisdered seperate form that content
  • They could be compared to editorial columns
  • While editoral columns are still part of the newspaper, they tend to share seperate ideas and opinions from the main articles
  • Nav tags can be used for sidebars and other small bits of information that while related to the content, are still different enough to necessitate seperation.
